LONDON

Enjoy the traditional delights of one of the world's most famous destinations...

The city with something for everyone

London is a city with something for everyone. If you love to shop, London's got you covered with some of the world's most premier shopping streets including Oxford Street, Regent Street and Knightsbridge, site of the famous Harrods department store. From designer clothes and jewellery to toys and tech, there's something to suit all tastes. Check out Hamleys in Regent Street for all the must have children's toys of the season and the Apple and Microsoft flagship stores located in Oxford Street.

If you're hungry after all of that shopping, there is a wealth of choice available in the city's restaurants, with just about every food type and culture catered for. From traditional, English fish & chips to Chop Suey in Chinatown, Soho you'll be spoilt for choice!

London also has an extensive night life and nightclub scene. Head to Shoreditch to dance the night away and to sample delicious cocktails in trendy bars.

And, of course, experience the magic of London's many other tourist attractions including the London Aquarium, the Madame Tussauds waxwork museum, the natural history museum, the London eye and many more! We challenge anyone to get bored in London!

Three places in London we highly recommend

HARRODS - Knightsbridge

Harrods motto is "Omnia Omnibus Ubique" which translates as "all things, for all people, everywhere" and they are certainly not exaggerating. With 330 departments spanning more than 1 million square feet of Retail space it would be difficult to think of a need that Harrods doesn't cater for. Peruse their extensive food halls for fine delicacies such as ostrich eggs and oysters, make yourself smell fabulous in their perfumery and look the part in Harrod's Egyptian styled clothing department.

THE LONDON EYE - Lambeth

This famous attraction is truly a photographer's dream, offering panoramic views of Westminster, Covent Garden, Soho and the course of the Thames river. At its peak, you'll be 135 meters in the air. Every year, on New Year's Eve, the London Eye is lit up with lights and fireworks are launched from around it at the stroke of midnight to welcome in the new year.

THE TOWER OF LONDON - Tower Hill

The Tower of London is a famous London landmark with a very rich history. During the 16th and 17th centuries, people including Elizabeth I and Sir Walter Rayleigh were imprisoned here. The tours of the tower led by the resident Yeomen (and their ravens) are absolutely fascinating, with one of the best parts of the tour being seeing the crown jewels worn by Her Majesty during such ceremonies as the state opening of parliament. There are also opportunities to see the dungeons used as prison cells for those unfortunate enough to be imprisoned there and rooms formerly used as banqueting and sleeping chambers by resident kings.
